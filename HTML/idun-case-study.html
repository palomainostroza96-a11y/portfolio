UX research and accessibility design for IDUN Minerals — creating an inclusive e-commerce experience that balances modern accessibility standards with the brand's minimalist Scandinavian aesthetic.

My Role: UX Researcher & Designer
Tools: Miro, Figma, Web Disability Simulator, Google Forms
Team: 4 UX Designers
Client: IDUN Minerals
Timeline: 6 Weeks (2025)
Focus: Accessibility, Sustainability, Marketing Experience

THE CHALLENGE

IDUN Minerals is a Swedish beauty brand focusing on natural and dermatologically tested products. Their previous website lacked accessibility features and emotional connection with users.

Our goal was to improve usability, highlight the brand's story, and increase online conversions through a complete redesign that prioritized user needs and modern web standards.

One of the main challenges was balancing WCAG accessibility standards with IDUN's minimalist and clean brand identity. This required thoughtful adjustments to spacing, typography, and element placement to maintain the brand's aesthetic while creating a more inclusive interface.

RESEARCH & PROCESS

Business Analysis

SWOT & PESTEL Analysis: Identified internal strengths and external challenges facing IDUN Minerals in the competitive beauty market.

Business Model Canvas: Mapped customer segments, value propositions, and key partners to understand the full business ecosystem.

Field Research

Pharmacy Visits: Conducted field studies at local pharmacies to study product placement, packaging visibility, and customer behavior.

Staff Interviews: Spoke with store staff to understand sales patterns and common customer questions about IDUN products.

User Research

Empathy Mapping & Personas: Created detailed customer persona (18-year-old social media user discovering IDUN via TikTok).

Accessibility Testing: Used Web Disability Simulator to test the website from perspectives of users with visual or motor impairments.

IN-STORE MARKETING STRATEGY

To increase visibility and sales of IDUN's products in pharmacies, we proposed innovative in-store initiatives:

QR Codes & Skin Analysis

QR codes on packaging and displays linking to IDUN's website, offering personalized skin quizzes and product recommendations.

Product Placement & Displays

Position products at eye level near checkout areas with clear signage. Highlight bestsellers on top shelves for maximum visibility.

Product Testers

Offer sample-sized versions of foundations, powders, and key products to encourage trial and purchases.

Digital Campaigns

Marketing through pharmacy newsletters, mobile apps, and targeted banner ads to reach customers before they visit.

Limited Editions & Bundles

Create campaigns with exclusive product bundles or multi-buy discounts to increase interest and customer loyalty.

Key Learnings

This project deepened my understanding that accessibility is not just about compliance — it's about creating an inclusive experience for all users. I learned how small UI adjustments — such as button size or heavier typefaces — can significantly improve usability.

I also realized the importance of balancing user needs with the integrity of a brand's visual identity. Accessibility and aesthetics are not mutually exclusive; they can enhance each other when approached thoughtfully.

Reflections

One limitation was that we did not have direct access to users with disabilities. Instead, we relied on simulations and heuristic evaluations, which provided valuable insights but also certain limitations.

If I were to revisit this project, I would involve users with real accessibility needs early in the design process and conduct usability testing with them to validate our solutions. This would ensure even more accurate and empathetic design decisions.

PERSONAL REFLECTIONS

This project deepened my understanding that accessibility is not just about compliance — it's about true inclusion. I learned how small UI changes, such as button size or typography weight, can have a significant impact on usability and user comfort.

What I also really enjoyed in this project was exploring creative marketing strategies and sustainable ideas — like recyclable packaging, limited editions and bundle offers, QR codes with personalized skin analysis, and more sample products. These were some of the concepts I contributed to, and they allowed me to combine user-centered thinking with business value and environmental awareness.

If I were to redo the project, I would involve users with accessibility needs earlier and conduct usability testing to validate our design decisions more deeply.
