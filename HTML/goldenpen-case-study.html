KONTROLLAVGIFT.SE

A high-fidelity prototype designed for Goldpen to streamline the process of managing parking violations and control fees through an intuitive, user-friendly digital platform.

My Role: UX Researcher & Designer
Tools: Miro, Figma, Google Forms
Team: 4 UX/UI Design Students
Client: Goldpen
Timeline: 3 Weeks (2025)
Type: High-fidelity Prototype

THE CHALLENGE

In collaboration with Goldpen, we developed a high-fidelity prototype for Kontrollavgift.se with the goal of improving the user experience, reducing incorrect disputes, and creating a more transparent and trustworthy journey.

During our first meeting with Joakim and Alexander from Goldpen, we gained insight into the company's workflow and the challenges they face. Based on their input, we were tasked with addressing four main issues:

Goldpen's service plays a unique role in managing parking fines on behalf of municipalities and private companies. However, their platform suffered from usability issues that led to confusion, unnecessary support requests, and frustration among users.

Our challenge was to design a modern, intuitive, and accessible experience that simplifies payments and disputes, while ensuring users understand when and how to use the service appropriately.

1

Reduce Irrelevant Disputes

Minimize incorrect questions and disputes sent to the error-reporting inbox

2

Clarify Service Purpose

Make it clear who the service is for and how it should be used

3

Encourage Valid Disputes

Guide users to dispute fines only when valid evidence exists

4

Improve Admin Experience

Use clearer wording and a more professional tone for administrators

RESEARCH & PROCESS

We began by structuring our workflow using the Double Diamond design process — Discover, Define, Develop, and Deliver.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths: Unique service offering QR code payments and strong municipal partnerships. In-house developers enable easier future improvements.

Weaknesses: Hard to navigate website with unclear language and visual hierarchy. Dispute examples often show negative outcomes, reducing user trust.

Opportunities: Expand to new cities, enhance design, and outgrow competitors with technical resources and broad coverage.

Threats: Larger financial players like Svea Bank and Hojab pose competitive risks with advanced technologies.

User Interviews & Testing

Each team member interviewed three users using the Think-Aloud method to explore how users perceived the service and how they would dispute a parking fine.

Key Insights: General confusion around terminology ("valid" vs "invalid dispute"), unclear navigation, and distrust toward QR codes were the main obstacles encountered.

Personas & Ideation

After analyzing interviews, we created key user personas representing typical parkers and their pain points.

Wireframing: We sketched wireframes for both desktop and mobile versions to visualize solutions that better guide users through payment or dispute actions.

USER TESTING & ITERATION

We conducted a second round of usability tests with eight participants to evaluate our prototype.

Common Issues Identified

  • Users were confused about the homepage and didn't know how to proceed
  • Unclear terminology around "valid" and "invalid dispute"
  • Distrust in QR codes led users to enter details manually
  • Some found the "Not Paid" button too alarming due to its color and placement

Iterative Changes Made

  • Replaced the "Not Paid" label with "Pay by [date]"
  • Clarified OCR scanning instructions with camera and bracket symbols
  • Simplified the dispute form and added info buttons explaining "valid/invalid dispute"
  • Introduced visual hierarchy improvements to reduce clutter and stress
