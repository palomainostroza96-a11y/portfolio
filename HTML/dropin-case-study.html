THE CHALLENGE

Lennart Svanberg approached us with a vision: to conceptualize an app that centralizes emergency help services and connects users to the right assistance instantly. The goal was to create a digital solution that simplifies access to urgent care, all in one place.

The app also needed to align with the course theme ‚Äî "The Future and the Impact of AI" ‚Äî ensuring that the concept would be forward-thinking, technically relevant, and sustainable for years to come.

RESEARCH & PROCESS

We began by exploring different directions, including Lennart's initial idea for a repair service app (for bikes or motorcycles). However, we identified a greater need among pet owners, a group often facing financial and emotional stress in emergencies.

Unlike human healthcare, veterinary care is not subsidized, and getting fast help can be complicated ‚Äî especially while traveling.

Market & Competitor Analysis

Our research revealed several key gaps:

  • No global app showing real-time availability for veterinary clinics.
  • No unified or digital medical record system for pets.
  • Telemedicine apps like Vetster, Airvet, and AskVet offer online consultations but no physical drop-in updates.
  • National networks (e.g., AniCura, Evidensia, Vets Now) are country-limited and require phone calls for emergencies.
  • Google Maps can show crowd levels but not emergency status or queue time.

To validate our findings, we built a Business Model Canvas (BMC) and SWOT analysis, which highlighted that social media marketing and API integration with clinics would be essential for success.

THE DESIGN
